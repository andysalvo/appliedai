# Voice Brief -- Applied AI Club

## Standard

The guest speaker program document (v1.2) is the voice standard. Everything the club writes should read like it was written by the same people.

The tone is professional and accessible. We write for adults because we want adults to take us seriously. We also write for students who are figuring out where they fit. Both audiences get the same voice: direct, honest, and specific.

## Principles

1. Write full sentences. Not fragments, not taglines, not marketing copy.
2. State facts and let them carry weight. If the club is good, the description will show it.
3. Acknowledge where we are. We are in our pilot semester. We are scheduling our first speakers. Honesty about our stage is more credible than pretending we are established.
4. Use "we" throughout. This is written by students, for anyone reading.
5. Describe what we do, not what category it falls into. "We run guest speaker sessions, hands-on workshops, and case competitions" is better than "Events and Programming."
6. No hype words. No "revolutionary," "game-changing," "transforming," "cutting-edge," "curated," or "empowering."
7. No em dashes. Use commas or periods.
8. No parallel structure cadences. "Built for X. Designed for Y. Made for Z." sounds generated.
9. No defining by absence. "Open to all students" instead of "No application required."
10. Professional warmth, not stiffness. We are serious about what we do and welcoming to anyone interested.

## Reference

This paragraph from the guest speaker program is the benchmark:

> "Most of our members are still early in their exposure to how AI and technology are used in professional settings. Guest speakers help bridge the gap between what students learn in the classroom and what professionals work with every day."

It is specific. It is honest about where our members are. It explains the value without overselling. Everything we write should meet this standard.

## Examples

Good: "The Applied AI Club is a student organization at Penn State dedicated to helping students understand how AI is changing the way businesses operate."

Bad: "Where Penn State students learn to think with AI."

Good: "For members who want to go deeper, Labs takes on full application builds using the same tools and workflows used in professional engineering teams."

Bad: "Our cutting-edge laboratory empowers students with hands-on AI innovation."

Good: "We maintain a list of AI tools worth knowing, organized for students at every level."

Bad: "Explore our curated collection of essential AI tools."
