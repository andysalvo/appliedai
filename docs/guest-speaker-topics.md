# Guest Speaker Topics

A reference for connecting us with the right professionals

The Applied AI Club is scheduling guest speakers for Fall 2026 at Penn State University Park. Our membership is primarily based in the Smeal College of Business, with students across accounting, finance, marketing, supply chain, MIS, risk management, and management. Below is a detailed list of the topics and professional backgrounds our members would benefit from most. Any introduction or referral is appreciated.

## Enterprise Systems and Business Platforms

- **ERP implementation and administration.** Professionals who work with SAP S/4HANA, Oracle, or Microsoft Dynamics 365 and can explain what these systems do, why companies depend on them, and what roles support them. SAP consultants, ERP project managers, and systems integrators are especially relevant given Penn State's #3 nationally ranked SCIS department.
- **CRM platforms and sales operations.** Salesforce administrators, HubSpot power users, or CRM strategists who can show students what a real CRM looks like in practice and how teams use it to manage pipeline, reporting, and customer data.
- **Cloud infrastructure and business technology.** IT professionals who manage cloud environments (AWS, Azure, GCP) for business applications, including data warehousing, SaaS deployment, or security.

## Data, Analytics, and Business Intelligence

- **Business intelligence and dashboard engineering.** Analysts or BI engineers who build reporting systems in Tableau, Power BI, or SAP Analytics Cloud that teams use to make decisions every day.
- **Data analysis and decision science.** Professionals who take raw company data, clean it, model it, and turn it into something a leadership team can act on. SQL, Python, and Excel are the tools most of our students are learning.
- **Data governance and quality.** Anyone responsible for how a company manages, stores, secures, or standardizes its data, particularly in regulated industries like finance or healthcare.

## Finance and Risk

- **Quantitative finance and trading technology.** Analysts, quants, or portfolio managers who work with algorithmic models, risk scoring, or trading platforms. Smeal operates the Rogers Family Trading Room and the Nittany Lion Fund.
- **Risk management and insurance analytics.** Actuaries, underwriters, or compliance professionals using predictive models for risk assessment. Penn State has one of the few dedicated risk management programs in the country.
- **Financial planning and analysis (FP&A).** Finance professionals who build forecasts, run scenario models, or use AI-assisted tools for budgeting and reporting at the corporate level.

## Accounting and Audit

- **Audit automation and continuous monitoring.** Auditors at firms or in-house teams who use AI or automation tools to scan transactions, flag anomalies, or accelerate the audit cycle.
- **Tax technology and compliance automation.** Tax professionals who use software or AI tools to manage filings, identify deductions, or streamline compliance workflows.
- **Forensic accounting and fraud detection.** Professionals who use data analysis or machine learning to identify financial irregularities or fraudulent activity.

## Marketing and Customer Intelligence

- **Marketing analytics and attribution.** Professionals who measure campaign performance, build attribution models, or use platforms like Google Analytics 4 to guide marketing spend.
- **Customer segmentation and personalization.** Marketers who use data to define audience segments, personalize outreach, or run A/B testing at scale.
- **Content strategy and AI-assisted production.** Marketing leaders who have integrated AI into their content workflows and can speak honestly about what works and what does not.

## Supply Chain and Operations

- **Demand forecasting and inventory planning.** Supply chain professionals who build or use forecasting models to manage inventory, procurement, or production scheduling.
- **Logistics optimization and transportation.** Professionals who work with route planning, warehouse automation, or distribution network design.
- **Procurement and supplier management.** Buyers, sourcing managers, or procurement analysts who use data to evaluate suppliers, manage spend, or assess supply chain risk.

## AI in Practice (Any Industry)

- **AI adoption and change management.** Leaders who introduced AI tools into an existing team or organization and can speak to the process, resistance, and outcomes.
- **AI product builders.** Founders or product managers who built a product with AI at its core and can walk through the decisions behind it.
- **AI governance and ethics.** Professionals responsible for AI policy, acceptable use guidelines, or bias auditing within their organizations. Aligns directly with Smeal's emphasis on responsible AI leadership.
- **Career changers.** Professionals whose roles were transformed by AI in the last few years, regardless of industry. These are often the most relatable speakers for students entering the workforce.

---

**What makes a great fit:** Technical depth is welcome but not required. What matters most is the ability to explain your work clearly to students who may have no background in your field. We value practitioners who can show what they actually do over executives giving high-level overviews. A 30 to 45 minute session over Zoom or in person works best.
